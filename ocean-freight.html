Ocean Freight

Primegate International engineers sea freight solutions from Dar es Salaam port to and from all major global trade lanes. We handle full container loads (FCL), less-than-container loads (LCL), break bulk and out-of-gauge cargo for importers, exporters and project owners across Tanzania and its landlocked neighbours.

No matter the size of your shipment, Primegate is committed to secure and on-schedule delivery. From port handling and customs clearance to inland haulage for transit cargo bound for Zambia, DRC, Rwanda, Burundi, Malawi and Uganda, every container and breakbulk piece is managed with end-to-end visibility.

Primegate integrates vessel space, port handling and inland legs into one accountable file. Whether you are feeding factories, projects or distributors, our Dar es Salaam control tower plans stuffing, sailing, customs clearance and last-mile delivery as a single sequence – not isolated moves.

Sea freight Tanzania – frequently asked questions

Which sea freight options does Primegate offer via Dar es Salaam port?

We handle full container loads (FCL), groupage and less-than-container loads (LCL), break bulk, roll-on/roll-off and project cargo via Dar es Salaam port, with onward road and rail connections into Tanzania and the region.

Can Primegate manage transit cargo for landlocked countries?

Yes. We arrange sea freight, port handling, customs clearance and inland haulage for transit cargo destined for Zambia, DRC, Rwanda, Burundi, Malawi, Uganda and other landlocked markets using established East and Central African corridors.

How far in advance should I book sea freight to Tanzania?

We recommend booking at least two to three weeks before your desired sailing date for FCL and earlier for break bulk or heavy lift cargo, so that vessel space, documentation and any special handling requirements are confirmed in good time.
